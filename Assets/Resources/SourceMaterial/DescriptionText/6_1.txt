In the third circle, the gluttonous wallow in a vile, putrid slush produced by a ceaseless, foul, icy rain – a great storm of putrefaction – as punishment for subjecting their reason to a voracious appetite. 
Cerberus, the monstrous three-headed beast of Hell, ravenously guards the gluttons lying in the freezing mire, mauling and flaying them with his claws as they howl like dogs. Virgil obtains safe passage past the monster by filling its three mouths with mud.
